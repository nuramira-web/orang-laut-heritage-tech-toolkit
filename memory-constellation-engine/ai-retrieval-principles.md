# AI Retrieval Principles

The Memory Constellation Engine may use AI to help users discover relationships across the archive.

## AI may help with

- suggesting related memories
- clustering themes
- identifying emotional patterns
- connecting places, rituals, and foodways
- generating search suggestions
- summarising approved materials
- helping users explore archive relationships

## AI must not

- invent stories
- create fake family histories
- reveal restricted materials
- override community interpretation
- treat uncertain material as fact
- flatten Orang Laut or Orang Pulau histories into one story

## Retrieval rules

The engine should only retrieve materials that are:

- approved for public use
- approved for educational use
- cleared for AI-assisted discovery
- not restricted or sensitive

## Good response pattern

When surfacing a connection, the system should say:

- what the connection is
- which source supports it
- whether it is confirmed or interpretive
- whether community review is needed

## Uncertainty language

Use:

“I can suggest a possible connection based on approved materials, but this should be reviewed by community contributors.”

Or:

“I do not have enough approved material to make that connection yet.”
