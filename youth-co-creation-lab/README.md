# Youth Co-Creation Lab
## Pulau Brani Project

The Youth Co-Creation Lab is a participatory heritage innovation and learning space within the Pulau Brani Project.

The lab brings together:
- students
- educators
- community members
- designers
- coders
- artists
- researchers
- storytellers
- technologists

to collaboratively explore how archives, oral histories, storytelling, participatory design, and AI may support cultural stewardship in the digital age.

The lab approaches heritage not as static information, but as:

- living memory
- emotional geography
- language
- ritual
- movement
- identity
- participation
- intergenerational continuity

---

# Core vision

The Youth Co-Creation Lab exists to help younger generations actively participate in the stewardship, interpretation, questioning, remixing, and transmission of cultural memory.

The lab encourages participants to:
- build things
- ask questions
- experiment critically
- collaborate ethically
- reflect on technology
- preserve nuance
- create new forms of public storytelling

The goal is not simply to digitise heritage.

The goal is to cultivate more thoughtful, participatory, creative, and ethically grounded relationships to memory in the AI era.

---

# Cultural stewardship literacy for the AI era

A core goal of the lab is to help build cultural stewardship literacy for the AI era.

As AI increasingly shapes how knowledge is:
- stored
- searched
- translated
- interpreted
- visualised
- summarised
- circulated

younger generations need opportunities to think critically about:

- archives
- consent
- bias
- storytelling
- authorship
- cultural representation
- emotional nuance
- digital stewardship
- community governance
- ethical AI design

The lab approaches AI not as a replacement for community memory, but as a set of tools that may support accessibility, participation, intergenerational learning, and creative exploration when guided by strong ethical principles.

---

# What happens in the lab

Participants may:
- conduct oral histories
- build memory constellations
- map emotional geographies
- document dialect and vocabulary
- test AI translation systems
- identify hallucinations and flattening
- remix archives creatively
- design heritage interfaces
- build archive tools
- create soundscapes and podcasts
- experiment with storytelling formats
- explore maritime memory and language

# Educational philosophy

The lab prioritises:
- participation over passive consumption
- creativity over memorisation
- reflection over technological hype
- stewardship over extraction
- collaboration over hierarchy
- inquiry over certainty

Students are encouraged not only to use AI tools, but to question them.

---

# Guiding principles

1. Community stewardship first  
2. Human review remains essential  
3. Preserve uncertainty honestly  
4. Creativity with care  
5. Accessibility across generations  
6. Preserve plurality and nuance  
7. AI should support, not replace  
8. Emotional truth matters  
9. Participation matters  
10. Heritage is relational, not only informational

# Long-term vision

The Youth Co-Creation Lab explores how younger generations may collaboratively steward memory, language, archives, and cultural futures in increasingly digital and AI-mediated worlds.

The lab treats heritage not as something frozen in the past, but as something continuously interpreted, negotiated, remixed, questioned, and carried forward.
