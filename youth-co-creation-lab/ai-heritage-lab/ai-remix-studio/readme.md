# AI Remix Studio

The AI Remix Studio is a creative and critical experimentation space within the Pulau Brani Project’s AI Heritage Lab.

Students use approved heritage materials — such as oral histories, photographs, recipes, maps, sounds, vocabulary, rituals, and family memories — to create new interpretive works with AI support.

The studio treats remixing as a form of cultural interpretation, not extraction.

## What students can create

- short-form video scripts
- podcast outlines
- soundscape concepts
- digital zines
- visual essay drafts
- interactive exhibit text
- memory collages
- speculative heritage futures
- social storytelling concepts
- archive playlists

## Core learning goal

Students learn not only how to use AI creatively, but how to evaluate what AI does to culture.

They ask:

- What did AI amplify?
- What did AI flatten?
- What did AI misunderstand?
- What became more accessible?
- What became less culturally specific?
- What should require human or community review?

## Core principle

AI may assist with creative interpretation, but it should not replace cultural context, contributor dignity, community consent, or human imagination.
