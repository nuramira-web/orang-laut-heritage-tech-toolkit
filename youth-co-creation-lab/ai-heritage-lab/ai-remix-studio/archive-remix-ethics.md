# Archive Remix Ethics

Archive remixing can make heritage more accessible and emotionally resonant, especially for younger audiences.

But remixing also carries risks.

## Risks

AI-assisted remixing may:

- flatten cultural nuance
- over-aestheticise displacement
- turn grief into content
- detach stories from community context
- invent details for narrative drama
- simplify dialect or ritual language
- make private memory feel public
- privilege viral formats over dignity

## Ethical questions

Before publishing or sharing a remix, ask:

- Was the source material approved for creative use?
- Is the contributor credited appropriately?
- Is cultural context preserved?
- Has anything sensitive been exposed?
- Did AI invent details?
- Did the remix change the emotional meaning?
- Would the contributor or community recognise this as respectful?
- Is this educational, extractive, or performative?

## Safer remix practices

Use:

- clear source attribution
- short context notes
- uncertainty language
- human review
- contributor review where possible
- community review for sensitive materials

Avoid:

- fake quotes
- invented family histories
- exaggerated trauma
- aestheticising poverty
- sacred material as visual decoration
- “ancient mystical” clichés
