# Workshop Activities

## Activity 1 — AI vs Human Interpretation

Students compare:

- an oral history excerpt
- an AI-generated summary
- a human interpretation

Questions:
- What nuance was lost?
- What assumptions appeared?
- What emotional texture disappeared?
- What should require human review?

---

## Activity 2 — Build a Memory Constellation

Students:
- select archive materials
- identify relationships
- map emotional themes
- create memory pathways

Outputs:
- diagrams
- digital maps
- constellation graphs

---

## Activity 3 — AI Hallucination Audit

Students intentionally test AI systems with:
- dialect words
- ritual practices
- maritime terms
- oral histories

Students identify:
- hallucinations
- flattening
- false certainty
- cultural bias

---

## Activity 4 — Emotional Heritage Mapping

Students analyse oral histories for:
- belonging
- loss
- pride
- longing
- resilience
- care

Students visualise emotional geographies across memories and places.

---

## Activity 5 — Heritage Chatbot Governance

Students design rules for:
- archive chatbots
- retrieval systems
- consent-aware AI
- culturally sensitive responses

Students debate:
- what the chatbot should refuse
- what requires review
- what should never become public

---

## Activity 6 — Disappearing Words

Students interview family members or elders about:
- old phrases
- sea terminology
- cooking language
- prayers
- jokes
- nicknames

Students record:
- pronunciation
- context
- emotional meaning
- generational differences
