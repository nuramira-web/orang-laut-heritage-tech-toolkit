# AI and Cultural Memory

Artificial intelligence increasingly shapes how information is:

- stored
- searched
- retrieved
- summarised
- translated
- interpreted
- circulated

This affects cultural memory.

---

# Cultural memory is not only data

Heritage involves:

- emotion
- ritual
- language
- atmosphere
- kinship
- spirituality
- movement
- embodiment
- uncertainty
- lived experience

AI systems may struggle with these forms of meaning.

---

# Risks

AI systems may:

- hallucinate
- oversimplify
- flatten dialect
- remove uncertainty
- misread emotion
- privilege dominant narratives
- separate stories from context
- misrepresent communities

---

# Opportunities

AI may also support:

- transcription
- translation
- accessibility
- archive discovery
- intergenerational learning
- language preservation
- relationship mapping
- emotional exploration
- participatory storytelling

---

# Stewardship questions

Students are encouraged to ask:

- Who owns memory?
- Who decides what becomes heritage?
- What should remain human-led?
- What should never be automated?
- How do we preserve plurality?
- How do we preserve dignity?
- What is responsible AI in cultural contexts?

---

# Final principle

The goal is not simply to apply AI to heritage.

The goal is to cultivate wiser relationships between technology, memory, community, and care.
