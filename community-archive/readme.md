# Pulau Brani Community Archive

The Pulau Brani Community Archive is a living, participatory archive documenting the memories, histories, objects, traditions, languages, foodways, and maritime knowledge connected to Pulau Brani and Singapore’s island communities.

The archive forms part of the broader Pulau Brani Project and is designed to support community-led stewardship, intergenerational storytelling, public education, digital heritage innovation, and responsible use of emerging technologies in heritage preservation.

## What the archive collects

The archive may include:

- oral histories
- family photographs
- recipes
- letters
- maps
- audio recordings
- objects
- fishing knowledge
- ritual and festival memories
- vocabulary and dialect terms
- village life memories
- migration and resettlement stories
- foodways and culinary practices

## Archive principles

1. Community stewardship over extraction
2. Consent before collection
3. Respect for sensitivity and nuance
4. Accessibility across generations
5. AI as support, not replacement
6. Preservation of context and voice
7. Openness to correction and reinterpretation
8. Respect for plural histories and identities

## Relationship to the Pulau Brani Project

This archive is a contribution and stewardship layer of the Pulau Brani Project. It supports the collection, review, organisation, and eventual public sharing of community memory.

Not all materials submitted to the archive will be public. Some may require family review, community review, or restricted access.
