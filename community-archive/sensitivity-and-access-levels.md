# Sensitivity and Access Levels

Not all heritage materials should be publicly accessible.

## Public

Materials approved for public viewing, education, and research.

Examples:

- general oral histories
- public photographs
- recipes
- maps
- educational materials

## Community Review Needed

Materials requiring review before public display.

Examples:

- emotionally sensitive stories
- disputed memories
- partially identified individuals
- culturally nuanced practices

## Family Restricted

Materials viewable only with family permission.

Examples:

- private letters
- family conflicts
- personal photographs
- intimate recordings

## Spiritually or Culturally Sensitive

Materials requiring particular care.

Examples:

- burial practices
- sacred rituals
- healing traditions
- restricted spiritual knowledge

## Temporary Restriction

Materials embargoed temporarily.

Examples:

- pending permissions
- incomplete review
- unresolved ownership questions

## AI Restrictions

Some materials may not be used for:

- AI training
- chatbot responses
- transcription systems
- public summarisation

Restrictions should always be respected.
