# Public Display Guidelines

Public display should make heritage accessible while respecting community dignity, privacy, sensitivity, and context.

The Pulau Brani Community Archive prioritises stewardship over extraction and spectacle.

---

# Before public display

Before publishing or exhibiting material, confirm:

- consent has been granted
- restrictions have been reviewed
- contributors understand where material will appear
- names and captions are accurate
- sensitive material has been assessed
- community review has occurred where appropriate

---

# Context matters

Materials should not be displayed without context.

Whenever possible, include:

- contributor credit
- date or approximate period
- location
- cultural context
- relationship to Pulau Brani or island communities
- associated oral histories or memories

The archive should preserve nuance rather than flatten stories into simplified narratives.

---

# Caption guidelines

Suggested format:

`Title. Contributor credit. Location. Approximate date. Short context.`

Example:

“Family gathering before Hari Raya. Courtesy of the Rahman family. Pulau Brani, approximately 1965. Shared as part of the Pulau Brani Community Archive.”

---

# Avoid sensationalism

Avoid:

- romanticising poverty
- presenting communities as frozen in time
- flattening diverse communities into one narrative
- using trauma for visual effect
- treating displacement only as nostalgia
- displaying sacred or private material casually

Community histories should be represented with dignity and complexity.

---

# Sacred and sensitive materials

Particular care should be taken with:

- burial sites
- healing practices
- spiritual rituals
- private family moments
- children
- restricted ceremonies
- emotionally sensitive stories

Some materials may require:

- restricted access
- family review
- contextual explanation
- non-public preservation only

---

# AI-generated material

If AI tools assist with:

- summaries
- translations
- captions
- transcription

human review should occur before public display.

AI-generated text should not invent context or certainty.

---

# Educational use

Public display may support:

- schools
- universities
- exhibitions
- community workshops
- intergenerational learning
- digital storytelling
- public heritage awareness

Educational framing should remain accessible and respectful.

---

# Online display

Digital publication increases visibility but also increases risk.

Before uploading materials online, consider:

- permanence
- copying and redistribution
- privacy
- searchability
- future misuse
- AI scraping

Some materials may be better suited to:
- limited access
- low-resolution display
- educational-only use
- in-person archives

---

# Attribution and acknowledgement

Contributors should be credited in ways they are comfortable with.

Possible approaches:

- full name
- family name
- initials
- anonymous
- community attribution

---

# Final principle

Public display should strengthen relationships between communities, memory, and future generations — not reduce heritage to content alone.
