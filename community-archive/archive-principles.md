# Archive Principles

The Pulau Brani Community Archive is guided by care, consent, dignity, and community stewardship.

## 1. Community first

Heritage belongs first to the communities who carry it. The archive should support communities in preserving and interpreting their own histories.

## 2. Consent before collection

Materials should not be recorded, copied, uploaded, published, translated, transcribed, or used with AI tools without clear consent.

## 3. Context matters

Stories, objects, images, and rituals should be documented with their social, cultural, family, maritime, religious, and historical context.

## 4. Not everything should be public

Some materials may be family-restricted, spiritually sensitive, culturally sensitive, or suitable only for community review.

## 5. AI should support, not replace, human stewardship

AI tools may assist with transcription, translation, tagging, and discovery. They should not invent, reinterpret, or override community knowledge.

## 6. Preserve plurality

The archive should not flatten Orang Laut, Orang Pulau, or island-descended communities into one single narrative.

## 7. Allow correction

Community members should be able to request corrections, restrictions, updates, or removal where appropriate.

## 8. Build for future generations

The archive should be accessible enough for young people, educators, researchers, and families to use and contribute to over time.
