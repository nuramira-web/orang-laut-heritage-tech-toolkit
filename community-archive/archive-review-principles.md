# Archive Review Principles

Archive review ensures that submitted materials are handled with care, accuracy, consent, and cultural sensitivity.

## Review questions

Before approving material for public use, reviewers should ask:

- Has consent been clearly given?
- Is the contributor authorised to share this material?
- Are any living individuals identified?
- Are family members, elders, or community members named?
- Does the material include spiritual, sacred, private, or sensitive knowledge?
- Is the context clear enough to avoid misrepresentation?
- Are place names, dates, and terms accurate?
- Should the material be public, restricted, or reviewed further?

## Review status options

- Pending
- Approved for public use
- Approved for educational use only
- Community review needed
- Family review needed
- Restricted
- Do not publish

## AI review

Before materials are used in AI tools, confirm:

- AI use was consented to
- restricted content is excluded
- summaries and tags are human-reviewed
- chatbot use is limited to approved public material
