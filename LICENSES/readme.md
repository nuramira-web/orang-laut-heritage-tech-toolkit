# Licenses Pack

This document contains suggested licenses and licensing guidance for the Orang Laut Heritage Tech Toolkit and associated cultural materials.

The project intentionally separates:

* software and code licenses
* documentation licenses
* cultural content permissions
* AI-use permissions
* community stewardship obligations

because conventional copyright alone is not sufficient for governing cultural memory.

# 3. Cultural Materials Notice

Suggested file:

`CULTURAL_MATERIALS_NOTICE.md`

```markdown
# Cultural Materials Notice

This repository may contain references to cultural materials, oral histories, language, ritual practices, family memory, maritime knowledge, photographs, recipes, or community archives connected to Pulau Brani, Orang Laut, Orang Pulau, and Singapore’s maritime communities.

Not all cultural materials are governed solely by conventional copyright.

Some materials may carry:

- family stewardship obligations
- community review requirements
- culturally sensitive restrictions
- spiritual or ritual sensitivities
- AI-use restrictions
- attribution expectations
- contextual obligations

Users of this repository are expected to:

- preserve contributor dignity
- respect contextual meaning
- avoid extractive use
- avoid flattening community complexity
- respect withdrawal requests
- respect layered permissions
- acknowledge uncertainty where appropriate

The Pulau Brani Project encourages users to treat cultural stewardship as an ongoing relational responsibility rather than a purely legal exercise.
```

# 4. AI Use Policy

Suggested file:

`AI_USE_POLICY.md`

```markdown
# AI Use Policy

The Pulau Brani Project explores the responsible use of AI in:

- archives
- oral histories
- storytelling
- retrieval systems
- translation
- emotional mapping
- language preservation
- educational tools

However:

AI-generated outputs are not treated as authoritative historical truth.

## AI principles

AI systems used in this project should:

- preserve uncertainty honestly
- remain source-grounded
- support human review
- preserve contributor dignity
- avoid hallucination
- avoid flattening communities
- avoid extractive storytelling
- preserve cultural nuance

## Restricted uses

Some archive materials may not be used for:

- AI training
- retrieval systems
- public summarisation
- remix systems
- chatbot systems
- automated interpretation

Restrictions should always be respected.

## Human review

All AI-generated outputs require human or community review before public publication.
```

# 5. TK Labels Notice

Suggested file:

`TK_LABELS_NOTICE.md`

```markdown
# TK Labels Notice

The Pulau Brani Project draws inspiration from the Local Contexts initiative and Traditional Knowledge (TK) Labels framework.

https://localcontexts.org/

TK Labels help communicate community-specific cultural permissions and stewardship expectations beyond conventional copyright.

These may include:

- attribution expectations
- family stewardship
- seasonal restrictions
- outreach permissions
- sacred or ritual sensitivities
- community authority
- non-commercial expectations
- AI-use restrictions

The project recognises that open access does not automatically mean unrestricted use.

Contributors, researchers, developers, and institutions are encouraged to engage respectfully with these stewardship expectations.
```

# 6. Archive Access and Permissions Notice

Suggested file:

`ARCHIVE_ACCESS_POLICY.md`

```markdown
# Archive Access Policy

Archive materials within the Pulau Brani Project may have different access levels.

## Public

Approved for public access.

## Educational Use Only

Approved for classroom, workshop, museum, or educational use.

## Community Review Needed

Requires review before publication or reuse.

## Family Restricted

Access controlled by contributors or family members.

## Culturally Sensitive

Requires additional review and contextual care.

## Spiritually Sensitive

May not be appropriate for public display or AI processing.

## AI Restricted

May not be used for:

- AI training
- retrieval systems
- summarisation
- remix systems
- chatbot interfaces

The project recognises that layered permissions are part of responsible cultural stewardship.
```
