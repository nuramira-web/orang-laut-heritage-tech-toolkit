# Presentation Structures

## 1. Memory → Meaning → Future

Useful for heritage storytelling.

- What memory or archive material exists?
- Why does it matter today?
- What future responsibility emerges?

---

## 2. Place → People → Practice

Useful for maritime and community heritage.

- What place matters?
- Who is connected to it?
- What practices shaped it?

---

## 3. Story → Reflection → Participation

Useful for Gen Z audiences.

- Tell a story
- Reflect critically
- Invite participation

---

## 4. Problem → Context → Stewardship

Useful for advocacy.

- What issue exists?
- What context matters?
- What forms of stewardship are possible?

---

## 5. Archive → Emotion → Public Meaning

Useful for oral history and exhibition presentations.

- What archive material exists?
- What emotional world does it carry?
- Why should the audience care?
