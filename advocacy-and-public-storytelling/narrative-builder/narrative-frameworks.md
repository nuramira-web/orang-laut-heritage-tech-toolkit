# Narrative Frameworks

The Narrative Builder may use several storytelling frameworks.

## 1. Memory → Meaning → Future

Useful for heritage storytelling.

Structure:

- What memory or history is being discussed?
- Why does it matter today?
- What future responsibility or possibility emerges from it?

---

## 2. Place → People → Practice

Useful for maritime, ritual, or community heritage.

Structure:

- What place is involved?
- Who are the communities connected to it?
- What practices, rituals, labour, or relationships shaped it?

---

## 3. Problem → Context → Stewardship

Useful for advocacy and public engagement.

Structure:

- What issue or challenge exists?
- What historical or emotional context matters?
- What forms of stewardship or action are possible?

---

## 4. Story → Reflection → Invitation

Useful for Gen Z and participatory workshops.

Structure:

- Tell a story
- Reflect critically
- Invite participation

---

## 5. Archive → Emotion → Public Meaning

Useful for exhibitions and oral history interpretation.

Structure:

- What archive material exists?
- What emotional world does it carry?
- Why should the public care?
