# Evidence and Story Balance

Strong public storytelling balances:

- emotion
- evidence
- memory
- context
- uncertainty
- public relevance

## Weak storytelling

Weak storytelling often:

- overclaims
- oversimplifies
- removes context
- universalises one experience
- exaggerates certainty
- uses emotion without evidence

## Strong storytelling

Strong storytelling:

- cites source material
- acknowledges uncertainty
- preserves community nuance
- explains why the issue matters
- connects memory to public meaning
- avoids flattening complexity

## Useful evidence types

- oral histories
- archive materials
- maps
- photographs
- recipes
- ritual descriptions
- vocabulary
- community testimony
- historical research
