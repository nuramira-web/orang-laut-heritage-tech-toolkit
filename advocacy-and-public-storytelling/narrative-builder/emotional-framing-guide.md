# Emotional Framing Guide

Emotion can help audiences connect to heritage and public issues.

But emotional storytelling should be used carefully.

## Productive emotional framing

- belonging
- care
- continuity
- resilience
- wonder
- intimacy
- responsibility
- intergenerational connection

## Risky emotional framing

- trauma spectacle
- pity narratives
- nostalgia without complexity
- romanticising poverty
- flattening grief
- exaggerated urgency
- “disappearing culture” clichés

## Questions to ask

- Is the emotional framing supported by the source material?
- Does it preserve dignity?
- Does it invite understanding rather than extraction?
- Does it allow complexity?
