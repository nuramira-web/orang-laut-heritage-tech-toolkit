# Advocacy and Public Storytelling

The Advocacy and Public Storytelling module helps communities, students, educators, and heritage practitioners communicate cultural memory, public issues, and community priorities clearly, ethically, and effectively.

This module is not designed to turn heritage into marketing.

It is designed to support civic storytelling, community representation, cultural stewardship, public communication, and ethical advocacy.

## Core tools

### 1. Narrative Builder

Helps users turn a memory, issue, place, or community concern into a clear public narrative.

It supports:

- key message development
- story structure
- historical context
- emotional framing
- evidence organisation
- calls to action

### 2. Audience Translator

Helps adapt the same issue for different audiences.

Examples:

- students
- policymakers
- community members
- funders
- museums
- teachers
- media
- researchers

### 3. Ethical Persuasion Checker

Reviews advocacy materials for risks such as:

- oversimplification
- sensationalism
- romanticisation
- trauma aesthetics
- flattening community complexity
- unsupported historical claims
- extractive storytelling

### 4. Presentation Builder

Helps users structure talks, workshops, school presentations, public briefings, and exhibition narratives.

It focuses on:

- narrative flow
- evidence
- emotional pacing
- audience engagement
- visual storytelling
- clear calls to action

### 5. Community Brief Generator

Turns archive findings, oral histories, workshop outputs, and youth reflections into short, accessible briefs.

These may support:

- public education
- policy engagement
- community updates
- school resources
- grant proposals
- institutional conversations

## Guiding principles

1. Community dignity first
2. Clarity without oversimplification
3. Emotional truth with factual care
4. Advocacy without extraction
5. Storytelling grounded in evidence
6. Audience awareness
7. Human and community review
8. AI as support, not authority

## Core idea

Good advocacy helps communities explain why memory matters, what is at stake, and what futures they want to build.
