# AI-Assisted Transcription Workflow

This guide explains how AI tools may be used to assist with oral history transcription, translation, and summarisation for community heritage projects.

The goal is to reduce administrative burden while preserving the accuracy, dignity, and context of community memory.

AI should support human stewardship — not replace it.

---

# Before using AI tools

Before uploading any recording to an AI transcription service:

- Confirm that the interviewee has given consent
- Explain how AI tools may be used
- Check whether the recording contains sensitive personal information
- Remove or redact material where necessary
- Respect any restrictions requested by the interviewee or family

If consent has not been given, do not upload recordings to external AI systems.

---

# Recommended workflow

## Step 1: Record the interview clearly

Best practices:

- Use a quiet environment where possible
- Place the recording device close to the speaker
- Avoid interrupting
- Record in the speaker’s preferred language or dialect
- Capture natural speech patterns and expressions

Recommended formats:

- WAV
- MP3
- MP4 (video)

---

## Step 2: Create secure backups

Immediately after recording:

- Save a local copy
- Save a backup copy
- Label files consistently

Example:

PB-OH-001_audio.mp3

---

## Step 3: Generate a first transcript

Possible tools:

- OpenAI Whisper
- MacWhisper
- Otter.ai
- Descript
- Google Recorder
- Other approved transcription systems

AI-generated transcripts should always be treated as drafts.

---

# Important limitations

AI systems may struggle with:

- Malay dialects
- Regional accents
- Mixed-language conversations
- Older speakers
- Maritime terminology
- Family names
- Religious or ritual language
- Code-switching between Malay, English, Hokkien, Javanese, Bugis, or other languages

Human review is required.

---

## Step 4: Human review and correction

A reviewer should:

- Correct names and places
- Fix cultural or historical inaccuracies
- Preserve original phrasing where possible
- Avoid over-cleaning natural speech
- Add contextual notes if necessary

The goal is to preserve voice, not create artificial formality.

---

## Step 5: Translation (optional)

Translations may improve accessibility for younger generations, educators, and researchers.

However:

- Some meanings may not translate directly
- Cultural concepts should not be flattened
- Original phrases may be preserved alongside translations
- Ritual or spiritual language may require contextual notes

Where possible, keep:

Original language → Translation

---

## Step 6: Create a summary

AI may help generate:

- Interview summaries
- Topic tags
- Timeline references
- Key themes
- Educational extracts

Suggested themes:

- Migration
- Maritime life
- Resettlement
- Foodways
- Rituals
- Religious practices
- Women’s labour
- Sea knowledge
- Festivals
- Childhood
- Kinship
- Fishing traditions

---

## Step 7: Archive responsibly

Recommended archive structure:

/oral-histories/
    /audio/
    /transcripts/
    /translations/
    /summaries/
    /photos/
    metadata.csv

---

# AI Use Principles

AI tools should:

- Support accessibility
- Reduce repetitive labour
- Improve discoverability
- Help younger generations engage with heritage

AI tools should not:

- Invent stories
- Replace human memory
- Remove cultural nuance
- Override community interpretation
- Generate fictional histories presented as fact
