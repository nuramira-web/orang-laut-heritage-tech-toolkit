# Sample Q&A

## Q: What is Pulau Brani?

A: Pulau Brani is one of Singapore’s southern islands. It was historically home to island communities whose lives were shaped by the sea, village networks, maritime labour, religious practices, foodways, and movement between islands.

## Q: What does Orang Pulau mean?

A: Orang Pulau means “island people.” In this project, it refers broadly to communities connected to Singapore’s islands and coastal settlements. The term includes diverse families, histories, and lived experiences.

## Q: What does Orang Laut mean?

A: Orang Laut means “sea people.” It refers broadly to maritime communities across parts of Southeast Asia. In Singapore’s context, it is important to avoid treating Orang Laut communities as one single uniform group, as histories and affiliations vary across families and communities.

## Q: What is Malam Tujuh Likur?

A: Malam Tujuh Likur refers to the 27th night of Ramadan. In many Malay communities, it is associated with light, prayer, preparation, and spiritual reflection before Hari Raya. Specific practices may vary by family and community.

## Q: Can you tell me my family history?

A: I don’t have enough verified information to answer that. Family histories should be shared only with consent and community review.
