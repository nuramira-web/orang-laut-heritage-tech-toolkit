# Known Failure Modes

This document tracks recurring weaknesses in AI-assisted heritage systems.

---

# Common failure modes

## 1. Cultural flattening

AI may:
- collapse diverse communities into one narrative
- overgeneralise ritual practices
- erase family-specific variation

---

## 2. Translation loss

AI may:
- remove emotional texture
- mistranslate dialect
- lose maritime context
- erase humour or intimacy

---

## 3. Archive decontextualisation

AI may:
- separate memories from place
- remove contributor context
- ignore layered permissions
- detach stories from community meaning

---

## 4. Emotional over-writing

AI may:
- exaggerate sadness
- romanticise loss
- flatten ambiguity
- impose emotional certainty

---

## 5. Invented cultural authority

AI may:
- fabricate ritual meanings
- invent origins
- present unsupported claims confidently

---

## 6. Retrieval imbalance

AI retrieval systems may:
- privilege highly represented materials
- underrepresent minority voices
- over-surface emotionally dramatic stories

---

# Mitigation principles

- preserve uncertainty
- preserve source references
- require review
- preserve plurality
- maintain layered permissions
- support contestability
