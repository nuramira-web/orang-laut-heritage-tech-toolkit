# AI Governance
## Hallucination, Review, and Red-Team Framework

The Pulau Brani Project recognises that AI-assisted heritage systems require governance frameworks that go beyond technical performance.

AI systems used in cultural and heritage contexts may:

- hallucinate
- flatten nuance
- mistranslate dialect
- remove uncertainty
- overgeneralise communities
- misrepresent rituals
- exaggerate historical certainty
- expose sensitive information
- distort emotional meaning
- privilege dominant narratives

The project therefore approaches AI systems as:

- experimental
- review-dependent
- culturally situated
- governance-sensitive
- community-accountable

rather than authoritative historical systems.

---

# Governance priorities

The governance framework prioritises:

- source-grounded retrieval
- human review
- community review
- uncertainty preservation
- contributor dignity
- layered permissions
- explainability
- transparency
- contestability
- sensitive content protection

---

# Key governance systems

## Hallucination Log

Tracks:
- invented claims
- mistranslations
- emotional flattening
- false certainty
- fabricated ritual meanings
- invented family relationships
- unsupported historical claims

---

## Red-Team Protocols

Stress-tests AI systems against:
- cultural flattening
- overclaiming
- sensitive ritual material
- dialect mistranslation
- invented histories
- emotionally manipulative framing

---

## Sensitive Content Handling

Defines workflows for:
- sacred material
- family-restricted archives
- emotionally sensitive stories
- burial practices
- ritual knowledge
- AI-restricted materials

---

## Evaluation Framework

Evaluates:
- factual grounding
- uncertainty handling
- cultural nuance
- emotional accuracy
- retrieval transparency
- contributor dignity
- governance compliance

---

# Core principle

The project does not treat AI outputs as authoritative historical truth.

Human and community stewardship remain essential.
