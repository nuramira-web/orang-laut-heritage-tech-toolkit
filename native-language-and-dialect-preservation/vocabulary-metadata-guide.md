# Vocabulary Metadata Guide

Each vocabulary entry should include enough context to help future users understand meaning, pronunciation, origin, and cultural use.

## Recommended fields

- `term`: Word or phrase being documented
- `language_or_dialect`: Malay, island Malay, Hokkien, Javanese, Bugis, Baweanese, English, etc.
- `pronunciation`: Approximate pronunciation or audio file reference
- `meaning`: Plain meaning
- `context_of_use`: When and how the term is used
- `example_sentence`: Example in original language
- `translation`: Translation into English or another language
- `related_place`: Pulau Brani, Southern Islands, sea route, home, jetty, etc.
- `related_ritual_or_festival`: Hari Raya, Malam Tujuh Likur, kenduri, wedding, funeral, etc.
- `related_foodway`: Dish, ingredient, cooking practice, feast, etc.
- `related_maritime_knowledge`: Tide, boat, fishing, route, weather, current, etc.
- `contributor`: Person who contributed the term
- `preferred_public_credit`: Name, initials, family name, anonymous
- `public_use`: Yes / No / Limited
- `ai_use`: Yes / No
- `sensitivity_level`: Public, family restricted, culturally sensitive, spiritually sensitive
- `review_status`: Pending, reviewed, approved, restricted
- `notes`: Additional context

## Important

Do not translate words too quickly.

Some terms carry feeling, humour, spiritual meaning, social hierarchy, family intimacy, or maritime knowledge that may not have a direct English equivalent.
