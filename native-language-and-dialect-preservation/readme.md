# Native Dialect and Vocabulary Archive

The Native Dialect and Vocabulary Archive documents words, phrases, expressions, sea terms, ritual language, food vocabulary, kinship terms, songs, pantun, prayers, and everyday speech connected to Pulau Brani, Orang Laut, Orang Pulau, and Singapore’s island communities.

Language carries memory.

This archive recognises that heritage is preserved not only in objects and photographs, but also in the way people speak, name places, describe the sea, call one another, cook, pray, joke, mourn, and remember.

## What this archive collects

- island Malay vocabulary
- maritime terms
- fishing and boat vocabulary
- food and cooking terms
- ritual and festival language
- kinship terms
- place names
- nicknames
- proverbs
- pantun
- prayers
- disappearing expressions
- multilingual phrases from Malay, Hokkien, Javanese, Bugis, Baweanese, Tamil, English, and other community languages

## Principles

1. Preserve original words where possible
2. Record pronunciation and context
3. Avoid flattening dialect into standard language
4. Respect sacred or private language
5. Allow uncertainty
6. Attribute community knowledge carefully
7. Support intergenerational learning
