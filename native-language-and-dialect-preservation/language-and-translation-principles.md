# Language and Translation Principles

The Dialect and Vocabulary Archive is guided by respect for linguistic nuance, community voice, and cultural context.

## 1. Preserve original language

Where possible, preserve the original word or phrase before translating it.

Original language often carries rhythm, humour, intimacy, and memory that translation may not fully capture.

## 2. Translate with context

A literal translation may not be enough.

Each term should include:

- meaning
- context
- emotional tone
- when it is used
- who uses it
- whether it is formal, intimate, humorous, sacred, or everyday

## 3. Respect dialect variation

Do not force all terms into standard Malay or standard English.

Island communities may have variations shaped by Malay, Bugis, Javanese, Baweanese, Hokkien, Tamil, English, Arabic, and other linguistic influences.

## 4. Allow uncertainty

If pronunciation, spelling, or meaning is uncertain, mark it clearly.

Use:

- “approximate”
- “uncertain”
- “family remembers this as…”
- “requires community review”

## 5. Sacred and restricted language

Some prayers, healing terms, ritual phrases, or spiritual expressions may not be suitable for public display.

These should be reviewed carefully before publication.

## 6. AI translation caution

AI tools may assist with translation, but human review is essential.

AI may miss:

- dialect
- code-switching
- humour
- kinship nuance
- ritual meaning
- maritime context
- old spellings
- oral pronunciation

## 7. Language as living heritage

The goal is not only to define words.

The goal is to preserve the worlds that words carry.
