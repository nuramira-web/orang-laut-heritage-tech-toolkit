# Pronunciation Archive

The Pronunciation Archive preserves the sound, rhythm, cadence, and oral texture of words, phrases, maritime terms, ritual language, food vocabulary, and family expressions connected to Pulau Brani, Orang Laut, Orang Pulau, and Singapore’s island communities.

This archive recognises that language is not only written meaning.

Pronunciation carries:

- memory
- humour
- intimacy
- migration
- generation
- place
- emotion
- community identity

## What this archive collects

- audio recordings of words and phrases
- pronunciation notes
- meanings
- example sentences
- emotional tone
- context of use
- speaker background, where consented
- related rituals, foodways, maritime practices, or oral histories

## Principles

1. Preserve original pronunciation where possible
2. Record variation rather than forcing one “correct” form
3. Respect family-specific and sensitive language
4. Do not publish sacred or restricted phrases without review
5. AI-generated transcription must be human-reviewed
6. Audio consent must be clear
